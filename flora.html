His first lesson

Silverleaf

Plate I

Argyrophyllum lene · the quiet mend

"Silverleaf for hurts of the flesh."

Habitat
Small gray-green patches hidden among the garden's vivid hues; easily overlooked, never far.
Preparation
A single leaf, pressed whole against the wound. Expect a sting, then nothing — not even a mark.
Virtue
Closes cuts, scrapes, and thorn-wounds within moments. Works no deeper than the skin.
In his hand

A little goes a long way, little one. Most true things do.

Moonpetal

Plate II

Petalum lunaris · the fever's ebb

"Moonpetal for fever."

Habitat
Pale cupped blooms along the night paths, keeping their own hours; shy of full daylight.
Preparation
Steep three petals in water warmed, never boiled. Give by the spoonful until the brow cools.
Virtue
Draws heat the way the moon draws tide — gently, and always toward rest.
In his hand

Fevers break at night. So does most stubbornness. Sit with her till morning anyway.

Sorrowroot

Plate III

Radix maeroris · the flower given twice

"Sorrowroot for wounds of the heart."

Habitat
Rare. It does not grow where it is convenient; it grows where it is needed.
Preparation
None. It is not brewed, ground, or pressed to the skin. It is given — from one hand into another. This is the whole of the preparation, and the hardest part.
Virtue
Works slowly, at the root of a hurt no leaf can reach. Often mistaken, at first, for an insult.
In his hand

One day someone will hand you this flower, and you will want to refuse it. Look at the hand a long moment before you do.
