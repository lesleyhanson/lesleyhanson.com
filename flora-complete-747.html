From the library beneath the garden

A Field Guide
to the Flora of Sopatra

Being a gardener's record of healing plants, kept for his daughter — with his notes in the margins.

Compiled from the notebooks of a keeper of the Vale
The Windsown Trilogy · Lesley Hanson

"Into every garden, a little rain must fall. It is the only way for things to grow."

His first lesson

Silverleaf

Plate I

Argyrophyllum lene · the quiet mend

"Silverleaf for hurts of the flesh."

Habitat
Small gray-green patches hidden among the garden's vivid hues; easily overlooked, never far.
Preparation
A single leaf, pressed whole against the wound. Expect a sting, then nothing — not even a mark.
Virtue
Closes cuts, scrapes, and thorn-wounds within moments. Works no deeper than the skin.
In his hand

A little goes a long way, little one. Most true things do.

Moonpetal

Plate II

Petalum lunaris · the fever's ebb

"Moonpetal for fever."

Habitat
Pale cupped blooms along the night paths, keeping their own hours; shy of full daylight.
Preparation
Steep three petals in water warmed, never boiled. Give by the spoonful until the brow cools.
Virtue
Draws heat the way the moon draws tide — gently, and always toward rest.
In his hand

Fevers break at night. So does most stubbornness. Sit with her till morning anyway.

Sorrowroot

Plate III

Radix maeroris · the flower given twice

"Sorrowroot for wounds of the heart."

Habitat
Rare. It does not grow where it is convenient; it grows where it is needed.
Preparation
None. It is not brewed, ground, or pressed to the skin. It is given — from one hand into another. This is the whole of the preparation, and the hardest part.
Virtue
Works slowly, at the root of a hurt no leaf can reach. Often mistaken, at first, for an insult.
In his hand

One day someone will hand you this flower, and you will want to refuse it. Look at the hand a long moment before you do.

Corpse flower

Plate IV

Flos rapax · the greedy bloom

Not for healing

"They take more than they give."

Habitat
The far edge of the forest, where the wild presses against the rose garden. It waits at borders. Mind which side you stand on.
Preparation
There is none recorded here, and none will be. Some knowledge is a door better left unmarked.
Virtue
It has one: it teaches, at great cost, the difference between what feeds a garden and what feeds on it.
In his hand

Promise me you will never go near them alone. — And if the day comes when you must: do not go as prey. Go as a gardener.

Shagbark hickory

Plate V

Carya solacium · the waiting tree

"For the waiting, and the ones who wait with you."

Habitat
Old stands on high ground. Recognized at once by its shaggy, generous bark.
Preparation
Steep the inner bark for a sweet, steadying tea. The resin may be chewed — a comfort much favored by boys, soldiers, and boys who became soldiers.
Virtue
Cures nothing. Mends nothing. Makes the night before a hard day survivable, which is its own medicine.
In his hand

If a stranger ever brews you this without being asked — pay attention. Tea is rarely about tea.

Memory bloom

Plate VI

Taraxacum memoriae · the field that remembers

"They hold echoes of joy and sorrow from the wider world."

Habitat
The white field beyond the orchard, where the blooms keep what the world could not bear to.
Preparation
Held, not harvested. One bloom to a hand. What you feel next was someone's, once — and now it is yours.
Virtue
Through them we learn compassion for struggles we have never faced. There is no stronger medicine in this guide.
In his hand

Count the blooms before you give them away. Then leave one wrapped in the twine. There is always someone still on the road.
